Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi is an Argentine professional footballer who plays as a forward for Inter Miami and the Argentina national team. Widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time, Messi has won a record eight Ballon d'Or awards and a record six European Golden Shoes.

Throughout his career, Messi has won numerous trophies including multiple UEFA Champions League titles and domestic championships with Barcelona. He led Argentina to victory in the 2022 FIFA World Cup, cementing his legacy as one of football's all-time greats.

Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo is a Portuguese professional footballer who plays as a forward for Al Nassr and captains the Portugal national team. Known for his incredible goal-scoring ability and athleticism, Ronaldo has won five Ballon d'Or awards and numerous other individual honors.

Ronaldo has achieved success with Manchester United, Real Madrid, and Juventus, winning multiple league titles and Champions League trophies. He holds the record for most goals in the UEFA Champions League and is the all-time leading scorer in international football.

Pelé

Pelé was a Brazilian professional footballer who played as a forward. Regarded as one of the greatest players of all time, he was among the most successful and popular sports figures of the 20th century. Pelé won three FIFA World Cups with Brazil, the most wins by any player.

During his career, Pelé scored over 1,000 goals and became a global ambassador for football. His technical skills, vision, and goal-scoring ability made him a legend of the sport, and he remains an iconic figure in football history worldwide.

Diego Maradona

Diego Maradona was an Argentine professional football player and manager. Widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the history of the sport, he was known for his dribbling ability, vision, and leadership on the field.

Maradona led Argentina to victory in the 1986 FIFA World Cup, where he captained the team and scored the famous "Hand of God" goal and the "Goal of the Century" against England in the same match. His club career included successful spells with Barcelona and Napoli.

Zinedine Zidane

Zinedine Zidane is a French former professional football player and manager. Known for his elegance, vision, and technical skills, Zidane is widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time. He won the 1998 Ballon d'Or and was named FIFA World Player of the Year three times.

Zidane led France to victory in the 1998 FIFA World Cup and UEFA Euro 2000. His club career included successful spells with Juventus and Real Madrid, where he scored a legendary volley in the 2002 UEFA Champions League final. As a manager, he led Real Madrid to three consecutive Champions League titles.
